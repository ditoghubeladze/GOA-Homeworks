About Fight Club




Fight Club is a 1999 film directed by David Fincher, based on the novel by Chuck Palahniuk. The story follows an unnamed narrator who, disillusioned with his consumer-driven life, forms an underground fight club with the charismatic Tyler Durden. As the club grows, it evolves into a rebellious movement that challenges societal norms, exploring themes of identity, masculinity, and freedom from materialism. The movie is known for its dark tone, intense psychological elements, and a memorable twist ending.
